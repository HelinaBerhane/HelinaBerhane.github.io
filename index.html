PhD Research Student

In September 2014 I began a PhD in geometric group theory, under the supervision of Dr. Andrew Duncan and Prof. Sarah Rees.

I'm planning to tackle the simultaneous conjugacy problem in Thompson's group V. My mathematical interests are:

  • Decision problems in group theory
  • The extended family of Thompson-like groups
  • Automata theory and formal languages
  • The Hanna Neumann conjecture (now theorem) and its generalisations
Outside of maths, I maintain a keen interest in computers and programming. I particularly enjoy tinkering with Python language, though I'm keen to learn more of C, Julia and Rust.

What I'm working on

Nathan Barker, Andrew Duncan and myself have submitted a paper which describes an algorithm to solve the power conjugacy problem in the Higman-Thompson groups Gn,r. This paper builds on Higman's work on the conjugacy problem.

As part of my contribution toward the paper, I have implemented the nitty-gritty details of this algorithm in a Python package called thompson. The package consists of two layers: a lower layer for working with the algebras Vn,r, and a higher layer for working with automorphisms of these algebras. For more information, see the GitHub repository, the project documentation, or the paper.
